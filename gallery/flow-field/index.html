How it’s made

Flow fields — particles advected through layered noise — are a classic generative technique; the variation here is that the field is anchored to a shape. Strands that drift too far are reeled back toward the nearest point of the cat, so the silhouette is always implied by the streaming cloud, never a hard cutout.

That shape logic is a pure module: it answers “is this point inside the cat?” and “where is the nearest point that is?” in normalised space, unit-tested and independent of canvas size.

The mask itself is baked offline — macOS Vision lifts Latte from a photo, the result is thresholded into an 80×85 bit grid and shipped as about a kilobyte of base64 — so the browser does no segmentation and the prerendered page stays static. Hold the pointer over the fur to break the wind.
